# Mark Surridge

**Full Name:** Mark Surridge

**Current Roles:** Director of tutor2u (1999–present), Business Studies educator, curriculum consultant

**Known For:** Co-founding tutor2u, developing innovative A Level Business revision resources, pioneering exam technique pedagogy for Business Studies, creating accessible digital content for Business education, developing the "chains of reasoning" approach to essay writing

## Biography

### Early Career and Educational Background

Mark Surridge began his career as a Business Studies teacher in the 1990s, working in secondary schools where he developed a keen understanding of the challenges students face when approaching A Level Business examinations. His early teaching experience highlighted significant gaps in available resources, particularly around exam technique and application skills—areas that would later become central to his professional mission.

### Foundation of tutor2u (1999)

In 1999, Mark Surridge co-founded tutor2u with Jim Riley, establishing what would become one of the most influential educational resource platforms in UK Business Studies education. The company began as a website providing free revision notes and teaching resources during the early days of educational digital content. Surridge recognised early the potential of online platforms to democratise access to high-quality Business Studies materials, making expert guidance available beyond traditional classroom boundaries.

### Development as an Educational Leader

Throughout the 2000s and 2010s, Surridge expanded tutor2u's offerings significantly, developing comprehensive resources across multiple specifications including AQA, Edexcel, OCR, and WJEC/Eduqas. His work has consistently focused on bridging the gap between theoretical business knowledge and practical exam performance. Under his leadership, tutor2u has become a trusted resource for thousands of teachers and tens of thousands of students annually.

Surridge has delivered numerous continuing professional development (CPD) sessions for teachers across the UK, focusing particularly on effective assessment preparation and pedagogical strategies for Business Studies. His training sessions often emphasise practical classroom techniques and evidence-based approaches to improving student outcomes.

### Recognition and Impact

By 2024, tutor2u resources reach an estimated 75% of UK A Level Business Studies students, making Surridge one of the most influential figures in contemporary Business education. His approach to making complex business concepts accessible whilst maintaining academic rigour has been widely praised by teachers, examiners, and education consultants.

## Key Ideas

### 1. Chains of Reasoning Methodology

Surridge's most significant pedagogical contribution is arguably the "chains of reasoning" approach to analytical and evaluative writing in Business Studies. This framework teaches students to construct extended arguments by linking points systematically:

- **Point:** Initial assertion or argument
- **Explain:** Detailed explanation of the business concept
- **Apply:** Specific application to the case study or context
- **Analyse:** Exploration of consequences and connections
- **Evaluate:** Judgement with justified reasoning

This structure directly addresses the Assessment Objectives (AOs) required by exam boards, particularly AO2 (Application), AO3 (Analysis), and AO4 (Evaluation). Surridge's approach helps students move beyond simple knowledge recall toward the higher-order thinking skills that distinguish top-grade responses. The methodology has been adopted widely in Business departments across the UK and features prominently in tutor2u revision materials.

### 2. Context Application Frameworks

Surridge emphasises that effective Business Studies answers must demonstrate strong contextual understanding. He developed specific strategies for extracting and applying case study information:

- **Pre-reading techniques:** Teaching students to annotate case studies systematically, identifying key stakeholders, business objectives, constraints, and opportunities
- **Context weaving:** Integrating specific case study details throughout answers rather than treating application as a separate element
- **Stakeholder analysis matrices:** Visual tools for understanding how business decisions affect different groups
- **SWOT and PESTLE contextualisation:** Adapting traditional frameworks specifically to case study circumstances

This emphasis on application addresses a persistent weakness identified by examiners across all major exam boards, where generic responses limit student achievement despite strong theoretical knowledge.

### 3. Specification-Aligned Content Architecture

Surridge pioneered the approach of creating resources meticulously aligned to specific exam board specifications. Rather than generic Business Studies content, tutor2u materials explicitly map to:

- Individual specification points from AQA, Edexcel, OCR, and WJEC
- Command word requirements specific to each exam board
- Mark scheme criteria and examiner expectations
- Specification updates and reforms

This precision has made tutor2u particularly valuable during periods of curriculum change, notably during the 2015 A Level reforms when Business Studies specifications underwent significant revision. Surridge's team produced comprehensive guidance helping teachers navigate new content requirements, assessment structures, and grading criteria.

### 4. Digital-First Resource Design

Surridge recognised early that effective educational resources must leverage digital affordances rather than simply replicating print materials online. His approach includes:

- **Modular content design:** Short, focused resources (typically 5-10 minutes) that address specific specification points
- **Multi-modal learning:** Combining text, video, infographics, and quizzes to accommodate different learning preferences
- **Regular updating:** Ensuring case studies and examples remain current with contemporary business practice
- **Mobile accessibility:** Resources optimised for student access on various devices

This digital pedagogy reflects understanding that contemporary students interact with content differently than previous generations, requiring flexible, accessible materials that support independent study.

### 5. Exam Technique Deconstruction

Surridge has extensively analysed exam technique requirements across Business Studies qualifications, breaking down seemingly complex skills into teachable components:

- **Command word taxonomies:** Clear explanations of what "assess," "evaluate," "analyse," and "justify" require in practice
- **Mark allocation strategies:** Teaching students to calibrate response depth to available marks
- **Time management frameworks:** Practical approaches to allocating exam time proportionally
- **Question stem analysis:** Training students to identify precisely what questions ask before constructing responses

His resources include detailed worked examples showing how strong responses are constructed, alongside examiner commentaries explaining why particular answers achieve specific grade boundaries.

### 6. Business Studies as Applied Social Science

Surridge advocates viewing Business Studies not merely as vocational preparation but as rigorous applied social science. This perspective emphasises:

- **Theoretical frameworks:** Business models and theories (Porter's Five Forces, Ansoff's Matrix, Mayo's Motivation Theory) as analytical tools
- **Evidence-based reasoning:** Using data and case study evidence to support arguments
- **Critical evaluation:** Questioning assumptions and considering alternative perspectives
- **Stakeholder complexity:** Recognising that business decisions involve ethical dimensions and conflicting interests

This approach elevates Business Studies within school curricula, positioning it as intellectually demanding and socially relevant—countering misconceptions that it represents "soft" content.

### 7. Formative Assessment Integration

Surridge's resources emphasise formative assessment as central to Business Studies pedagogy:

- **Knowledge retrieval practice:** Regular low-stakes quizzes reinforcing key concepts
- **Diagnostic questioning:** Identifying specific misconceptions about business concepts
- **Practice question banks:** Extensive collections allowing deliberate practice of exam skills
- **Self-assessment rubrics:** Tools enabling students to evaluate their own responses against mark schemes

This approach reflects understanding of research by Dylan Wiliam and others on effective assessment for learning, adapted specifically for Business Studies contexts.

### 8. Teacher Professional Development Focus

Recognising that resource quality depends partly on effective pedagogical implementation, Surridge has consistently invested in teacher development:

- **CPD webinars:** Regular online training sessions covering specification changes, exam technique teaching, and effective resource use
- **Teacher communities:** Facilitating professional networks where Business Studies teachers share practice
- **Exemplar materials:** Providing model lesson plans and teaching sequences
- **Assessment guidance:** Helping teachers understand mark schemes and examiner expectations

This commitment to teacher development distinguishes tutor2u from purely content-focused providers.

## Pedagogical Approach

### Constructivist Foundations

Surridge's methodology reflects constructivist learning theory, emphasising that students must actively build understanding rather than passively receive information. His resources encourage:

- **Active engagement:** Quizzes, activities, and practice questions requiring student response
- **Scaffolded progression:** Building from foundational concepts toward complex analytical skills
- **Application opportunities:** Regular practice linking theory to real business contexts
- **Metacognitive development:** Encouraging students to reflect on their own understanding and technique

This approach aligns with research by cognitive psychologists including John Sweller (cognitive load theory) and Robert Bjork (desirable difficulties), though Surridge typically frames his pedagogy in practitioner-accessible language rather than academic terminology.

### Explicit Instruction Philosophy

Whilst embracing active learning, Surridge advocates for explicit instruction in exam technique and analytical frameworks. This reflects understanding that novice learners benefit from clear guidance rather than pure discovery approaches. His resources typically:

- Model expert thinking through worked examples
- Provide clear success criteria for different response types
- Offer structured frameworks (like chains of reasoning) as thinking scaffolds
- Gradually reduce support as student competence develops

This balanced approach acknowledges research by Paul Kirschner, John Sweller, and Richard Clark on the limitations of minimally guided instruction for novice learners.

### Curriculum Coherence

Surridge emphasises building coherent understanding where business concepts interconnect rather than existing as isolated facts. His resources often:

- **Link themes across topics:** Showing how, for example, leadership theories connect to organisational culture and change management
- **Integrate functional areas:** Demonstrating that business decisions involve marketing, finance, operations, and people management simultaneously
- **Apply consistent frameworks:** Using similar analytical approaches across different contexts
- **Build cumulative knowledge:** Designing resources that reference and build upon previously encountered concepts

This approach reflects understanding that expertise involves developing rich, interconnected knowledge schemas rather than accumulating discrete facts.

### Differentiation Strategies

Recognising diverse student needs, Surridge's materials incorporate differentiation through:

- **Tiered resources:** Different complexity levels addressing the same specification points
- **Multiple modalities:** Text, video, audio, and visual resources accommodating learning preferences
- **Extension materials:** Additional challenges for high-achieving students
- **Foundational support:** Glossaries, definitions, and basic explanations for those building understanding

This inclusive approach aims to support all students whilst stretching high achievers.

### Assessment for Learning Integration

Formative assessment permeates Surridge's pedagogical approach:

- **Frequent low-stakes testing:** Regular quizzes supporting knowledge retention
- **Immediate feedback:** Digital resources providing instant response to answers
- **Self-assessment tools:** Rubrics and exemplars enabling student evaluation of their work
- **Diagnostic identification:** Resources helping identify specific misconceptions or skill gaps

This emphasis reflects the assessment for learning research of Black and Wiliam, adapted for Business Studies contexts.

## Critical Perspectives and Debates

### Teaching to the Test Concerns

Some educators express concern that resource platforms like tutor2u, with their explicit exam focus, encourage "teaching to the test" rather than deeper business understanding. Critics argue this might:

- Narrow curriculum breadth beyond specification requirements
- Reduce intellectual curiosity about business as a discipline
- Prioritise technique over genuine analytical capability
- Create instrumental approaches to learning focused primarily on grades

Surridge would likely counter that effective exam technique requires genuine understanding, and that his resources develop transferable analytical skills applicable beyond examination contexts. The "chains of reasoning" approach, for instance, teaches structured argumentation valuable across academic and professional contexts.

### Digital Dependency Questions

The prominence of digital resources raises questions about:

- Whether students develop sufficient independent thinking without structured frameworks
- Screen time implications for learning and wellbeing
- Digital divide concerns for students with limited technology access
- Over-reliance on external resources potentially undermining classroom teaching

These concerns reflect broader debates about technology in education rather than criticisms specific to Surridge's work, but merit consideration given tutor2u's extensive reach.
