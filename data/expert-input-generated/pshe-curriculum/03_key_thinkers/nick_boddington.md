# Nick Boddington

**Full Name:** Nicholas (Nick) Boddington

**Current Role:** Independent consultant in PSHE education; Former Development Manager at the PSHE Association

**Known For:** Leading authority on drug and alcohol education in schools; contributions to PSHE curriculum development; pioneering evidence-based approaches to substance use education; development of practical teaching resources for PSHE practitioners

---

## Biography and Career History

Nick Boddington has been a central figure in UK PSHE education for over two decades, with particular expertise in drug and alcohol education. His career has spanned classroom teaching, local authority advisory work, and national curriculum development, making him one of the most influential practitioners in the field.

### Early Career and Teaching Background

Boddington began his career as a secondary school teacher, where he developed practical expertise in delivering PSHE education in challenging contexts. His classroom experience informed his later work, ensuring that his curriculum development remained grounded in pedagogical reality rather than theoretical idealism.

### PSHE Association Leadership

Boddington served as Development Manager at the PSHE Association (formerly the PSHE Association for Teachers), where he played a pivotal role in shaping the organisation's approach to curriculum guidance and teacher support. During his tenure, he was instrumental in developing the Association's framework for drug and alcohol education, which has become the standard reference point for schools across England.

His work at the PSHE Association involved:
- Leading the development of curriculum guidance documents
- Providing expert consultation on policy developments
- Delivering training to teachers and school leaders nationwide
- Contributing to the Association's responses to government consultations on PSHE and health education

### Independent Consultancy Work

Following his time at the PSHE Association, Boddington has worked as an independent consultant, continuing to support schools, local authorities, and organisations in developing effective PSHE provision. His consultancy work has included curriculum auditing, staff training, and the development of bespoke resources for specific contexts.

## Key Ideas and Contributions

### 1. Evidence-Based Drug and Alcohol Education

Boddington has been a consistent advocate for evidence-based approaches to drug and alcohol education, challenging ineffective or potentially harmful practices. He has emphasised the importance of:

- **Moving away from 'shock tactics'**: Boddington has been critical of fear-based approaches that exaggerate risks or use graphic imagery, arguing that such methods can be counterproductive and may increase curiosity rather than promote informed decision-making.

- **Challenging misinformation**: He has stressed the need for factually accurate information about substances, their effects, and patterns of use, arguing that exaggeration undermines credibility with young people.

- **Focus on decision-making skills**: Rather than simply providing information about drugs, Boddington advocates for developing young people's capacity to assess risks, manage peer influence, and make informed choices.

### 2. The 'Learning Outcomes Approach' to PSHE Planning

Boddington has championed a learning outcomes approach to PSHE curriculum planning, emphasising what pupils should know, understand, and be able to do by the end of their education. This approach involves:

- **Clear progression**: Mapping how learning develops from Key Stage 1 through to Key Stage 4, with age-appropriate content and increasing complexity.

- **Skills development**: Identifying specific skills (such as critical thinking, decision-making, and communication) that should be developed across the PSHE curriculum.

- **Measurable outcomes**: Creating assessment frameworks that allow teachers to evaluate whether learning objectives have been achieved, addressing the historical challenge of assessment in PSHE.

### 3. Spiral Curriculum Design in PSHE

Boddington has articulated the importance of spiral curriculum design in PSHE, where topics are revisited at different key stages with increasing depth and sophistication. His framework includes:

- **Age-appropriate introduction**: Introducing concepts at the earliest appropriate stage, using accessible language and relevant contexts.

- **Progressive development**: Building on prior learning with each revisit, adding new dimensions and complexity.

- **Connection to lived experience**: Ensuring that curriculum content connects to pupils' developing experiences and concerns at each stage.

### 4. 'Normalisation' Concerns in Prevention Education

A significant contribution from Boddington has been his analysis of the risk of 'normalisation' in drug and alcohol education. He has highlighted how poorly designed lessons can inadvertently:

- Suggest that substance use is more common than it actually is among young people
- Create a perception that 'everyone does it', which can increase rather than decrease likelihood of experimentation
- Fail to acknowledge that most young people do not use illegal drugs, and that many delay alcohol use

His guidance emphasises the importance of presenting accurate prevalence data and affirming positive choices.

### 5. Harm Reduction within Educational Settings

Boddington has contributed to nuanced discussions about harm reduction approaches in schools. While recognising the controversy, he has argued for:

- **Realistic acknowledgement**: Accepting that some young people will experiment despite education, and ensuring they have information to reduce risks.

- **Balanced messaging**: Combining clear guidance about law and school rules with practical safety information.

- **Safeguarding integration**: Ensuring that harm reduction approaches are integrated with safeguarding procedures and support for vulnerable pupils.

### 6. Addressing Commercial Influences and Marketing

Boddington has been vocal about the need for PSHE education to address commercial influences on young people's choices, particularly regarding alcohol. His work includes:

- Analysis of marketing techniques used by alcohol companies
- Development of media literacy approaches to help young people critique advertising
- Guidance on exploring the alcohol industry's targeting of young adults and the 'pre-loading' culture

### 7. Teacher Confidence and Professional Development

Recognising that effective PSHE delivery depends on confident, skilled teachers, Boddington has prioritised:

- **Subject knowledge development**: Providing teachers with accurate, up-to-date information about drugs, alcohol, and related legislation.

- **Pedagogical techniques**: Training in interactive teaching methods appropriate for sensitive topics, including distancing techniques and ground rules.

- **Managing disclosure**: Equipping teachers to handle situations where pupils disclose substance use or related concerns, linking to safeguarding procedures.

### 8. Partnership Working with External Agencies

Boddington has provided guidance on effective use of external contributors in PSHE education, particularly in drug and alcohol education where police officers, health professionals, or recovery organisations may be involved. His framework emphasises:

- Teachers retaining ownership of learning outcomes
- Ensuring external contributors enhance rather than replace planned curriculum
- Briefing visitors to ensure messages are age-appropriate and evidence-based
- Evaluating the impact of external input on pupil learning

## Pedagogical Approach

### Philosophical Foundations

Boddington's pedagogical approach is rooted in several key principles:

**Respect for Young People's Agency**: His work consistently emphasises young people's capacity to make informed decisions when provided with accurate information and opportunities to develop critical thinking skills.

**Evidence-Based Practice**: A commitment to grounding educational approaches in research evidence about what works, rather than assumption or tradition.

**Developmental Appropriateness**: Careful attention to cognitive, emotional, and social development in designing learning experiences.

### Teaching Methodologies

Boddington advocates for interactive, participatory teaching approaches in PSHE, including:

**Distancing Techniques**: Using scenarios, role play, and case studies that allow pupils to explore issues without personal disclosure, creating psychological safety while enabling meaningful engagement.

**Critical Thinking Activities**: Structured approaches to analysing media messages, evaluating sources of information, and exploring multiple perspectives on controversial issues.

**Skills Rehearsal**: Opportunities for pupils to practise decision-making, assertiveness, and communication skills in safe, supported environments.

**Group Work and Discussion**: Carefully structured collaborative activities that allow pupils to learn from peers while developing social skills.

### Assessment Approaches

Boddington has contributed to evolving understanding of assessment in PSHE, arguing for:

- **Baseline and endpoint assessment**: Establishing what pupils know and can do at the start of a unit, and measuring progress.

- **Formative assessment**: Using ongoing assessment to inform teaching and adjust approaches to meet pupil needs.

- **Skills-based assessment**: Evaluating pupils' ability to apply knowledge and skills, rather than simply recalling information.

- **Self and peer assessment**: Developing pupils' capacity to evaluate their own learning and provide constructive feedback to others.
