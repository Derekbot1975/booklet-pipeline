# Cara Flanagan

**Full Name:** Cara Flanagan

**Current Roles:** Educational author, psychology textbook writer, teacher trainer

**Known For:** Co-authoring the leading AQA A Level Psychology textbooks, developing student-centred resources that have shaped Psychology teaching across the UK, and creating accessible learning materials that translate complex psychological concepts for secondary school students

## Biography and Career History

Cara Flanagan is one of the most influential figures in UK Psychology education at secondary level, having shaped how A Level Psychology is taught for over two decades. Her career has been devoted to making psychology accessible, engaging, and pedagogically sound for both teachers and students.

Flanagan's background combines academic psychology with practical teaching experience, enabling her to bridge the gap between university-level psychology and secondary education. She began developing educational resources for A Level Psychology in the late 1990s, at a time when the subject was growing rapidly but lacked comprehensive, curriculum-specific textbooks designed specifically for the UK examination system.

Her partnership with fellow author Rob Liddle has produced some of the most widely used Psychology textbooks in UK schools and colleges. Their collaboration began with the Oxford University Press and continued through multiple specification changes, adapting to the demands of different AQA Psychology specifications from 2008 onwards.

Flanagan's work extends beyond textbook authorship. She has been instrumental in providing continuing professional development (CPD) for Psychology teachers, delivering training sessions at conferences organised by the Association for the Teaching of Psychology (ATP) and creating supplementary resources that support teachers in delivering complex content effectively.

Her contribution is particularly significant given the challenges of teaching Psychology at A Level. Unlike subjects with long traditions in secondary education, Psychology faces unique pedagogical challenges: it requires scientific rigour whilst dealing with content that students often approach with misconceptions from popular culture; it demands both breadth (covering multiple approaches) and depth (understanding research methodology and evaluation); and it must prepare students for potential university study whilst remaining accessible to those studying it as a standalone subject.

## Key Ideas and Contributions

### 1. The 'Complete Companion' Approach to Textbook Design

Flanagan pioneered what became known as the 'Complete Companion' series for AQA Psychology. This approach represents a fundamental philosophy about how textbooks should support learning. Rather than presenting psychology as a series of facts to memorise, the Complete Companion books are structured to guide students through the specification systematically whilst encouraging active engagement with material.

The 'Companion' concept means the textbook walks alongside the student's learning journey rather than simply presenting information. Each spread is carefully designed with consistent structural features: specification content is clearly signposted, key terms are highlighted and defined, research studies are presented in accessible formats, and evaluation points are explicitly structured using frameworks like PEEL (Point, Evidence, Explain, Link).

This design philosophy responds to cognitive load theory by breaking complex topics into manageable chunks, using dual coding (combining text with visual representations), and providing clear signposting that reduces extraneous cognitive load. Flanagan's books consistently use colour coding, text boxes, and visual hierarchy to help students navigate complex information.

### 2. Specification-Led Content Organisation

Flanagan's textbooks are meticulously aligned with AQA examination specifications. This seems obvious, but her execution goes beyond simple content matching. Each section directly corresponds to specification entries, using the exact terminology and scope defined by AQA. This specification-led approach helps teachers plan lessons efficiently and gives students clarity about examination expectations.

The 2015 specification reform (implemented from 2015, first examined in 2017) represented a significant challenge, introducing compulsory topics in research methods, mathematical requirements, and a reformed approach to approaches in psychology. Flanagan's response to these changes demonstrated her deep understanding of curriculum design, reorganising content to reflect the new specification's thematic structure whilst maintaining pedagogical coherence.

Her treatment of research methods exemplifies this approach. Rather than presenting methodology as an isolated topic, Flanagan integrates methodological understanding throughout the textbooks, ensuring students encounter research methods in context whilst also having dedicated sections for systematic coverage. This dual approach responds to AQA's assessment model, where research methods questions can appear across all examination papers.

### 3. The APFCC Framework for Evaluation

One of Flanagan's most significant contributions to Psychology pedagogy is her systematic approach to teaching evaluation skills. A Level Psychology requires students to demonstrate critical thinking by evaluating theories, research studies, and applications. This is notoriously challenging for students transitioning from GCSE, where evaluation is typically less sophisticated.

Flanagan developed the APFCC framework (and variations thereof) to scaffold evaluation writing:
- **A**pplication/Appropriateness
- **P**ositives (supporting evidence)
- **F**urther points (alternative theories or explanations)
- **C**ounterarguments (limitations or criticisms)
- **C**onclusion

This framework provides students with a mental structure for constructing balanced, sophisticated evaluative arguments. The approach acknowledges that evaluation is not simply about listing strengths and weaknesses but about constructing an argument that considers evidence from multiple perspectives.

Her textbooks model this approach explicitly, with evaluation sections that demonstrate how to develop points effectively, link evidence to arguments, and draw reasoned conclusions. This pedagogical scaffolding has been adopted by Psychology teachers nationally and represents a practical solution to one of the subject's most significant teaching challenges.

### 4. Making Research Accessible Through Structured Summaries

Flanagan recognised that understanding psychological research is fundamental to A Level Psychology success, but original research papers are often inaccessible to 16-18 year olds due to statistical complexity, specialist terminology, and sheer length. Her solution was to create structured research summaries that preserve scientific accuracy whilst presenting studies in formats students can understand and remember.

Each research study in her textbooks follows a consistent format: aims, procedures, findings, and conclusions are clearly separated; sample details are highlighted; methodological features are made explicit; and ethical considerations are noted where relevant. This structured approach helps students develop a schema for understanding psychological research, making it easier to remember studies and apply them in examinations.

Importantly, Flanagan maintains scientific integrity in these summaries. She does not oversimplify to the point of distortion, instead carefully selecting the most pedagogically relevant aspects of research whilst preserving the essence of methodology and findings. This balance between accessibility and accuracy is a hallmark of her work.

### 5. Student-Centred Learning Activities

Flanagan's textbooks consistently incorporate activities that promote active learning rather than passive reading. These activities reflect contemporary understanding of effective learning strategies from cognitive psychology, including retrieval practice, elaborative interrogation, and self-explanation.

Activities include:
- **Exam-style questions** embedded throughout chapters, providing distributed practice
- **Application exercises** that require students to use psychological concepts to explain real-world scenarios
- **Evaluation tasks** that scaffold critical thinking
- **Research method activities** that help students understand methodology through practical engagement
- **Revision summaries** structured to promote retrieval practice

This student-centred approach reflects Flanagan's understanding that learning requires active cognitive engagement. The activities are carefully designed to align with assessment objectives, ensuring that practice activities develop the specific skills required for examination success.

### 6. Supporting Teacher Workload Management

Flanagan has consistently demonstrated awareness of teacher workload pressures. Her resources are designed to reduce planning time whilst maintaining high-quality teaching. The comprehensive nature of the Complete Companion textbooks means teachers have a reliable, specification-aligned resource that can form the backbone of their curriculum delivery.

Beyond the textbooks themselves, Flanagan has supported the development of teacher resources including PowerPoint presentations, worksheets, and assessment materials. These resources acknowledge the reality that many Psychology teachers are non-specialists or are teaching Psychology alongside other subjects, and therefore need high-quality, ready-to-use materials.

Her approach to teacher support extends to professional development. Through conferences and training sessions, Flanagan has helped teachers understand specification changes, develop effective pedagogical approaches, and navigate the challenges of teaching Psychology effectively.

### 7. Addressing the Breadth Challenge in A Level Psychology

A Level Psychology covers an extraordinary breadth of content: biological, cognitive, developmental, social, individual differences, and psychopathology approaches; multiple topic areas within each approach; research methodology; and mathematical skills. This breadth creates significant pedagogical challenges.

Flanagan's solution involves careful curriculum mapping that ensures comprehensive coverage whilst avoiding overwhelming detail. Her textbooks distinguish between content students must know in depth (for extended writing questions) and content requiring broader understanding (for shorter answer questions). This differentiation helps students allocate revision time effectively.

She also emphasises thematic connections across topics, helping students see how different areas of psychology interrelate. For example, discussions of research methods are connected to specific studies; ethical considerations appear in context; and debates (nature-nurture, free will-determinism, holism-reductionism, idiographic-nomothetic) are woven through relevant topics rather than treated as entirely separate content.

## Key Publications

### Core Textbooks

**Flanagan, C., & Liddle, R. (2015).** *AQA Psychology for A Level Year 1 & AS: Student Book.* Illuminate Publishing.
This textbook covers the first year of the two-year A Level course (or the complete AS Level). It includes detailed coverage of social influence, memory, attachment, approaches in psychology, psychopathology, and research methods. The book was written specifically for the 2015 specification reform and represents Flanagan's most pedagogically sophisticated work, incorporating feedback from over a decade of previous textbook development.

**Flanagan, C., & Liddle, R. (2016).** *AQA Psychology for A Level Year 2: Student Book.* Illuminate Publishing.
This second-year textbook completes the A Level coverage with biopsychology, research methods (advanced), issues and debates, relationships, schizophrenia, aggression, forensic psychology, eating behaviour, stress, and addiction. The book maintains consistency with the Year 1 volume whilst addressing the increased sophistication expected in the second year.

**Flanagan, C., & Liddle, R. (2018).** *AQA Psychology for A Level Year 1 & AS: Revision Guide.* Illuminate Publishing.
This revision guide distils the Year 1 content into a format specifically designed for revision and examination preparation. It emphasises retrieval practice, includes examination tips, and provides model answers that exemplify good examination technique.

**Flanagan, C., & Liddle, R. (2018).** *AQA Psychology for A Level Year 2: Revision Guide.* Illuminate Publishing.
The companion revision guide for Year 2 content, maintaining the same pedagogical approach whilst addressing the more complex material in the second year of study.

### Earlier Editions

**Flanagan, C., & Liddle, R. (2008).** *AQA(A) Psychology AS: The Complete Companion.* Oxford University Press.
This was the first 'Complete Companion' textbook, written for the specification that began in 2008. It established the design principles and pedagogical approach that would characterise all subsequent textbooks.

**Flanagan, C., & Liddle, R. (2009).** *AQA(A) Psychology A2: The Complete Companion.* Oxford University Press.
The A2 (second year) companion to the 2008 AS textbook, completing the A Level course under the modular specification system that preceded 2015 reforms.

**Flanagan, C., & Liddle, R. (2012).** *AQA(B) Psychology AS: The Complete Companion.* Oxford University Press.
Flanagan also wrote textbooks for the alternative AQA Specification B, demonstrating her versatility and comprehensive understanding of different approaches to organising Psychology curricula.

### Supporting Resources

**Flanagan, C., & Liddle, R. (Various dates).** *Complete Companions: Teacher Resource Packs.* Oxford University Press and Illuminate Publishing.
These comprehensive resource packs include lesson plans, PowerPoint presentations, worksheets, and assessment materials designed to complement the textbooks.

**Flanagan, C., & Liddle, R. (2019).** *AQA Psychology for A Level Workbook.* Illuminate Publishing.
This workbook provides additional practice activities, including examination-style questions, application exercises, and research methods tasks.

## Influence on Curriculum and Policy

### Shaping Pedagogical Practice

Flanagan's textbooks have become the de facto standard in many UK schools and colleges teaching AQA Psychology. Market research suggests that her Complete Companion series has been adopted by the majority of centres offering AQA Psychology A Level. This widespread adoption means Flanagan's pedagogical choices—her frameworks for evaluation, her selection of research studies, her organisation of content—have fundamentally shaped how Psychology is taught at secondary level.

Her influence extends beyond AQA centres. Teachers following other specifications (Edexcel, OCR, WJEC) often reference Flanagan's textbooks for their clear explanations and structured approach, even when adjusting content for different specification requirements.

### Supporting Specification Development

Whilst Flanagan has not directly written examination specifications, her work has influenced their practical implementation. Her textbooks provide feedback to examination boards about what is pedagogically viable and how specification content can be organised for effective teaching. The iterative process of specification development, textbook writing, and teacher feedback creates a dialogue in which Flanagan's voice has been significant.

Her response to the 2015 specification reforms demonstrates this influence. The reforms introduced significant changes, including linear assessment (replacing modular examinations), increased emphasis on research methods and mathematics, and compulsory coverage of issues and debates. Flanagan's textbooks helped teachers navigate these changes by providing clear structures for the new content and demonstrating how different specification elements could be integrated coherently.

### Professional Development Contributions

Flanagan has contributed to teacher professional development through:
- Presentations at Association for the Teaching of Psychology (ATP) conferences
- Training sessions for teachers implementing new specifications
- Written guidance on teaching challenging topics
- Supporting new-to-subject teachers through comprehensive, user-friendly resources

Her contributions to CPD have been particularly important given the shortage of specialist Psychology teachers in many UK schools. Psychology A Level has grown enormously in popularity (it is now one of the most popular A Level subjects), but many teachers come to the subject from other disciplines or teach it as a minority subject alongside their main specialism. Flanagan's resources provide these teachers with the subject knowledge and pedagogical guidance they need to teach effectively.

### Influence on Examination Performance

While difficult to quantify directly, Flanagan's textbooks likely influence national examination performance in AQA Psychology. The structured approaches to answering questions, the explicit teaching of evaluation skills, and the comprehensive coverage of content all support students in meeting assessment objectives effectively.

Her modelling of examination technique—including sample answers, examiner tips, and guidance on command words—helps students understand what examiners expect. This demystification of the assessment process is particularly valuable for students from backgrounds with limited cultural capital around examination success.

## Pedagogical Approach

### Cognitive Psychology Principles

Flanagan's pedagogical approach reflects principles from cognitive psychology, particularly regarding memory, learning, and expertise development:

**Dual Coding**: Her textbooks consistently combine verbal information with visual representations. Diagrams, photographs, flowcharts, and tables complement written explanations, supporting students with different learning preferences and reducing cognitive load.

**Chunking and Organisation**: Complex topics are broken into manageable sections with clear hierarchical organisation. This chunking helps students encode information more effectively and retrieve it more easily during examinations.

**Retrieval Practice**: Activities and questions are distributed throughout chapters, promoting the testing effect. Revision guides emphasise active recall rather than passive re-reading.

**Elaborative Interrogation**: Application activities and evaluation tasks encourage students to ask 'why' and 'how' questions, promoting deeper processing of information.

**Worked Examples**: Model answers and structured guidance provide worked examples that help students develop expertise in answering examination questions.

### Constructivist Elements

Despite the structured nature of her textbooks, Flanagan incorporates constructivist principles:

**Active Learning**: Activities require students to engage actively with content rather than passively receiving information.

**Scaffolding**: Complex skills like evaluation are carefully scaffolded, with frameworks gradually withdrawn as students develop competence.

**Building on Prior Knowledge**: New concepts are explicitly connected to prior learning, both within Psychology and from students' general knowledge.

**Zone of Proximal Development**: Activities are pitched at appropriate challenge levels, with support provided to enable students to access content just beyond their current capability.

### Assessment for Learning

Flanagan's resources incorporate assessment for learning principles:

**Clear Success Criteria**: Specification statements and assessment objectives are made explicit, so students understand what success looks like.

**Formative Assessment Opportunities**: Embedded questions and activities provide opportunities for formative assessment that can guide subsequent teaching.

**Feedback-Oriented Design**: Model answers and examiner guidance help students understand how to improve their work.

**Self-Assessment**: Revision resources promote self-assessment and metacognitive awareness.

### Inclusivity and Accessibility

Flanagan's textbooks demonstrate awareness of inclusivity and accessibility:

**Readability**: Language is clear and accessible without patronising students or compromising academic integrity.

**Visual Design**: Layout uses principles of universal design for learning, with clear visual hierarchy, generous white space, and consistent formatting that supports navigation.

**Cultural Diversity**: Research examples and applications include diverse cultural contexts and researchers from various backgrounds.

**Gender Representation**: Case studies and examples avoid gender stereotyping and represent diverse gender identities.
